about

I graduated in 2014 from the National University of Asunción, Paraguay, where I studied Informatics Engineering while working part time at the Centro Nacional de Computación. I also had the chance to work on speech recognition for the Sugar Project as a Google Summer of Code project during my last year in college.

After graduation, I worked for a year and a half with open data as a developer for the Governance and Democracy Program of USAID/CEAMSO. Additionally, I collaborated with Juan Pane in several open data projects and co-founded a software startup with a group of friends (who happen to be top-notch engineers), we go by the name of Codium these days.

Having become a data junkie, I am currently enrolled in the EIT Digital Masters in Data Science program, doing my first year at Eindhoven University of Technology. I have recently (February 2016) begun an internship at Philips Research, where I am working on a deep learning project.

During my free time I enjoy traveling and creative reading/writing, you can find some evidence of the latter (in Spanish) at my tumblog.
